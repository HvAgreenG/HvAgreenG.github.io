Digitalization and its CO2 footprint

The digitalization transformation uses our energy sources through the infrastructure of computer servers, networks and datacenter that it works on. It is unclear what the energy use and the CO2 emission of various digital activities is. When we want to use digital technology, it is of interest to know what the impact is on the environment, and what the alternatives are.

According to CO2 calulator by using compressed images, efficient file formats and lightweight fonts, avoiding bloated frameworks we can make websites more eco-friendly. That inspires us to set up an experiment.

  • Define a webpage with contents of only text, images, videos, ... .
  • Using this calculator, measure how many CO2 is produced, in terms of grams, everytime someone visits this given page.
  • Increase the size of the page, in terms of kilobytes (kB) without changing the type of the content and repeat the experiment.
The goal of the abovementioned steps is to gather data and use them to obtain a function with which we are able to determine the amount of CO2 produced everytime someone visits a given webpage (this webpage may contain different contents such Amsterdamtext, images, video, ...).
